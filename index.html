Does A NHL Team's Success Increase Prices?

Over the past two years, we have seen a dramatic change in ticket prices compared to the past 100 years, is the NHL getting greedy?

By: Michael Crisci

A "Rich" History

Over the past 100 years, the NHL has strived to bring the very best in hockey entertainment to people around the world. From the Original Six teams that started it all, to the 32 franchises that run the league today, it has been an absolute wild ride for the NHL. Over the years, the NHL has seen lockouts, pandemics, 19 different team relocations and foldings, and 30 expansion teams. The NHL is trying to spread the outreach of hockey everywhere, including Europe, where the NHL has hosted international games similar to the NFL in order to gain viewers. With all these new markets, the NHL feels they have successfully brought hockey to the people—or at least they thought they had.

Hockey is for Everyone? Yeah Right!

Over the past two years, NHL ticket prices have skyrocketed, almost doubling for most teams. For a league that has been around for 100 years, there hasn’t been an increase like this one. Sports analyst Michael d’Amore, a Falk College alumnus, says that dynamic pricing is the main reason for the change. This pricing is impacted by a range of factors, from team success to the worth of the franchises and even the taxes that these teams pay. All of these factors have made prices comparable to a family vacation for some.

“It definitely isn’t inflation or household income,” said d’Amore. “NHL ticket prices increased because teams discovered demand was inelastic, corporate and tourist buyers set the clearing price, and expansion franchises normalized premium pricing across the league.”

Inelastic Demand

Over the past decade, NHL teams have been increasing their ticket prices in hopes of making the most money possible. D’Amore said that since teams don’t have a cap when it comes to selling tickets, they will sell tickets at monumentally high prices to draw people in.

“It’s really annoying for us NHL fans,” said d’Amore. “Teams increase their prices about $3.37 per win!.”

For those who don’t know, dynamic pricing is a pricing strategy that uses analytics such as team success and prior data to set prices based on supply and demand. There is obviously very high demand for NHL tickets, which is why we are seeing such high prices.

Corporations and Tourism

When it comes to corporations, many corporations try to invest their money in well-known and successful franchises. A key example of this is the Original Six teams. These teams have many sponsors, such as Molson, Canadian Tire, Scotiabank, Dunkin’, and even FanDuel. When teams have sponsors like this, they raise prices and use their corporate power to bring people in, which results in increases like this.

The Original Six teams have been increasing their prices monumentally, and it has an effect not just on corporations, but on tourists as well. As stated earlier, the NHL has been expanding hockey to new markets like Europe, and this has brought in many tourists as the NHL continues to highlight its Original Six teams. These teams then make money off tourists who would pay almost anything to see one of these teams play.

Expansion Pricing

The NHL has also been expanding its league in efforts to bring in more money and gain new markets of fans. In the past 15 years, the NHL has gained three new teams and one team that was relocated. The NHL has received the Winnipeg Jets, Vegas Golden Knights, and the Seattle Kraken. They have also received the Utah Mammoth, which was a team relocated from the poorly run Arizona Coyotes franchise. Vegas, Seattle, and Utah, being the more recent teams, have seen a surge in high pricing, as you can see above, and they continue to grow as their franchises grow. There is no ceiling for these new teams, and they feel that since they are new, they are allowed to take advantage of their fans with premium pricing, labeling fans as being “part of history.” All of these teams have seen increases from 2024.

Conclusion

To conclude, the NHL will continue to use dynamic pricing to impact fans, concessions, and organizations for years to come. This year was a wake-up call for the NHL, as ticket prices were almost doubled for most franchises. If the NHL wants to continue to thrive, they are going to reach a point where fans will no longer pay these absurdly high prices, and there will be major consequences from a sudden drop, similar to the rapid increase. The NHL may hit somewhat of a recession in the foreseeable future regarding the way it spends its money.

“I can tell you one thing, and that is these sudden increases made by the NHL on tickets are going to have a major impact within the next decade,” said d’Amore. “I really do hope that the NHL switches back to its original pricing and really hones in on its mission statement that hockey is for everyone.”
